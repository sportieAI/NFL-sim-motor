In a thrilling matchup at Arrowhead Stadium, the Kansas City Chiefs defeated the San Francisco 49ers 21-17 in a hard-fought contest. 

The Chiefs controlled the game early, taking a 14-10 lead into halftime behind strong passing attack that accumulated 245 yards through the air. The 49ers mounted a comeback in the third quarter, outscoring KC 7-0 to take a 17-14 lead.

However, the Chiefs showed their championship mettle in the fourth quarter, scoring a crucial touchdown to reclaim the lead at 21-17. The Chiefs' defense stepped up when needed, forcing two turnovers while only giving up one.

KC finished with 365 total yards compared to SF's 342, with a balanced attack featuring 245 passing yards and 120 rushing yards. The 49ers relied heavily on their passing game with 278 yards but struggled on the ground with only 64 rushing yards.

The Chiefs converted 42% of their third down attempts and were efficient in the red zone, capitalizing on 75% of their scoring opportunities. This victory showcases the Chiefs' ability to win close games and their continued dominance at home.
